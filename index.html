About Me

My name is Elizabeth Taddeo, I am a senior SMAD major at James Madison University.

I have loved storytelling ever since I could remember. I started putting on shows and acting at a very young age. I would use my mom’s old MacBook computer to make small mini-series with my younger brother. I then was introduced to an iPad and saw that storytelling could be told anywhere. Then, I received my first camera, and I told an endless number of stories.

Short Films, Documentaries, 30-sec Edits, Video Diaries

Storytelling is such a beautiful thing. It brings people together. It’s what I’ve always wanted to do.

Graphic Design

I approach each project with a focus on understanding the client’s vision and goals. Through collaboration and creativity, I strive to create designs that not only meet expectations but also inspire and engage audiences. I prioritize usability and aesthetics, ensuring that every piece I create is both visually striking and functional.

I have developed a strong proficiency in Adobe Photoshop, which allows me to create eye-catching visuals and manipulate images with confidence. I enjoy exploring various techniques, from photo retouching and compositing to digital painting.

Photography

Photography, to me, is about connection—connecting with my subjects, the environment, and the story I aim to tell. I strive to create images that not only showcase beauty but also convey a deeper narrative. Each photograph is an opportunity to express a unique perspective and highlight the extraordinary in the ordinary.

Film

I believe that storytelling is at the heart of filmmaking. My approach combines strong visual aesthetics with engaging narratives, allowing me to create films that not only entertain but also provoke thought and emotion.
